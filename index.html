What goes into the score

Every stock is scored across eight independent pillars. Each pillar contributes to a final composite score (0–100). The AdaptiveLearner module re-weights the pillars over time based on which ones actually predicted closed-trade P&L — so the engine learns from your real outcomes, not just from training data.

PILLAR 01

Quality & fundamentals

Piotroski F-Score (9-point), Altman Z-Score (5-factor), accrual ratio, ROE / ROIC trend, quality composite.

PILLAR 02

Valuation

P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA vs sector medians. PEG. Earnings yield vs 10-year. Sector-adjusted Z-scores.

PILLAR 03

Momentum & trend

12-1 momentum, RSI, MA crossovers, breakout detection, relative strength vs sector and SPY.

PILLAR 04

Risk & volatility

Beta, downside deviation, drawdown profile, idiosyncratic volatility, tail risk estimates.

PILLAR 05

Regime detection

VIX-based classifier: CRISIS / HIGH_VOL / NORMAL / LOW_VOL. Position sizing and pillar weights adjust by regime.

PILLAR 06

ML ensemble

XGBoost + LightGBM, TimeSeriesSplit CV, SHAP feature importance, walk-forward retraining.

PILLAR 07

Sentiment

NLTK VADER tuned with a finance lexicon; optional FinBERT (1.7 GB HuggingFace model) for deeper sentiment.

PILLAR 08

Position sizing

Kelly criterion with correlation adjustment, max-position caps, sector-concentration limits.

Honest about what it isn't

Caveats we tell users upfront

A scoring model is a decision aid, not a crystal ball. Here is what Sandman doesn't claim to do.

It's not a robo-advisor BY DESIGN

Sandman ranks and scores. It does not place trades, manage allocation across accounts, or rebalance automatically. Final decisions are yours.

It won't predict black swans EVIDENT

The ML ensemble is trained on historical regimes. Genuinely novel events (2020 March, war shocks, AI-coup style breaks) will hit Sandman's blind spots the same way they hit every other model.

It's only as good as the data REAL

yfinance has occasional gaps. Some financial statements lag. The regime detector is reactive, not predictive. We surface these honestly in the dashboard rather than hide them.

Sample size is small FAIR

Even a 20-year backtest gives you ~5,000 trading days. That sounds like a lot but covers only 2-3 full macro regimes. Treat the backtest as a sanity check, not a guarantee.
